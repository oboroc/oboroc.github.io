Legal status and principal activities

The joint stock commercial Victoriabank was established in the Republic of Moldova in 1989. The Bank was re-established as joint stock commercial bank on 26 August 1991.

Victoriabank is a comprehensive joint stock lending establishment, offering a complete set of banking operations and services to small- and medium-sized enterprises. The principal goal of the Bank is to promote, support and finance private business and enterpreneurship, as well as create the conditions essential for the transformation from a central planed to market economy within the Republic of Moldova.

As at 31 December 1997, the Bank has eight branches in Moldova, located in Chisinau, Balts, Tiraspol, Floreshti, Soroka, Nisporeni, Kaushany and Orhei.

Victoriabank has received a blanket licence to execute operations in foreign currencies. It established correcpondent relations with a number of international banks.

Principles of presentation

General

The financial statements for the year 1997 have prepared in accordance with International Accounting Standards.

The Bank maintains its accounting records in accordance with the banking regulations prevailing in Moldova and prepares its statutory financial statements on such basis. Adjustments have been made in order to present the financial statements in accordance with International Accounting Standards.

The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost convention, except for the tangible fixed assets. In accordance with the regulations as set by Ministry of Finance in Moldova, Victoriabank revalued the fixed assets by applying indices to the cost price of certain categories of fixed assets.

The financial statements have been prepared after the statutory appropriation of profit. All amounts are stated in Moldovan Lei unless otherwise noted.

Principles of consolidation

The 1997 financial statements include the figures of the branches in Chisinau, Balts, Floreshti, Soroka, Nisporeni, Kaushany and Orhei but not the Tiraspol branch. Previously, this was due to the uncertain political and economical environment in the Trans-Dniester region.

Inflation

The rate of inflation for 1997 amounted to approximately 11.2% (1996: 15%).

Economical and political risks affecting the Bank

The Bank faces a number of risks and challenges due to the political and economical instability in Moldova as a result of the transition from a centrally planed economy to a market economy. They may have a potentially negative effect on the financial position and operations of the Bank.

Accounting principles

General

Assets and liabilities are started at nominal value, unless mentioned otherwise.

Foreign currency translation

Assets and liabilities denominated in foreign currencies are translated to Lei at year-end exchange rates. The official exchange rate at year-end was Lei 4.66 - USD 1 (1996: Lei 4.65 - USD 1). Transactions and the resulting income and charges in foreign currencies are converted at the rate applicable on transaction date. Exchange rate differences arising from the conversion of assets and liabilities are included in the profit and loss account.

Due from banks

Advances to banks are valued at nominal value less a provision for doubtful debts, if considered necessary.

Loans and advances to customers

Loans and advances to customers are stated at nominal value, after deduction of specific provisions for doubtful debts and a general loan loss provision.

The specific provision for doubtful debts is calculated by classifying credit exposures into certain categories of recoverability. The estimated net realisable value of the collateral involved has been considered in determining the provisions for doubtful debts.

A general loan loss provision has been formed to cover the general inherent risks in relation to existing loans which, although not separately identified, are known to be present in any portfolio.

Charges to the profit and loss account for those provisions are included in the "Losses on loans and advances".

In 1997 both growth of credits and improvement of relations between the Bank and borrowers took place. They were marked by: a variety of crediting subjects and objects, use of different forms of security for repayment of credits, strengthening of control over the intended use of granted funds, and improvement of crediting methods. The Bank paid special attention to strengthening of credit relations with permanent customers having their settlement accounts with "Victoriabank".

Taking into account the role which credits play in the formation of earnings they are given primary consideration when determining priorities of activities. Approach of the Bank to the credit policy is aimed at attainment of maximum efficiency with an allowance of the admissible risk level. It is achieved based on through investigation of the borrower's financial position, knowledge of the market and forecasting of its development.

Interest bearing securities

Interest bearing securities are stated at nominal value, except for State treasury bills wich are valued at the acquisition price plus accrued interest.

Investments

Investments are carried at the lower of cost or market value.

Tangible fixed assets

Tangible fixes assets are carried at cost or adjusted value less accumulated depreciation. Fixed assets are partly revalued on the basis of instructions of the Moldavian Ministry of Finance, which publishes the indices to be applied to certain categories of fixed assets. The revaluation of the fixed assets has been posted to the Revaluation reserve.

Furniture and equipment is depreciated on a straight-line basis, over its expected economic life, which varies from 2 to 5 years. The depreciation of buildings is calculated on a straight-line basis over its expected economic life of 40 years.

Operating income and expenses

Income and expenses are attributed to the year to which they relate.

Interest income and commisions are not stated as income if the collection of the interest and commision is considired doubtful.

Taxation

Taxes have been calculated in accordance with Moldovan accounting and taxation principles.

Financial instruments

Introduction

As defined by IAS 32 (Financial Instruments: Disclosure and Presentation) a financial instrument is any contract that gives rise to both a financial assets of one enterprise and a financial liability or equity instrument of another enterprise.

Within the balance sheet of the Bank, the majority of financial assets and liabilities are captured by this definition. Off-balance sheet items such as letters of credit and guarantees are also included in the definition. IAS 32 requires certain disclosure on the risks and fair values associated with financial instruments.

Interest rate risk

Changes in the market interest rates have a direct impact on the contractually determined cash flows associated with some financial assets and liabilities, when these bear interest rates which are periodically reset to market rates, and on the fair value of others for which the interest rates are fixed throughout the period of the contract.

The residual maturity of assets and liabilities is presented in the notes to the balance sheet. Substantially almost all financial assets and a smaller part of financial liabilities are interest bearing. Rates are set at or around current market levels, using requirements set by and guidance from the National Bank of Moldova. Most of the Bank's assets and liabilities reprise within one year and interest percentages can in many cases be changed upon the Bank's discretion. Accordingly, there is little risk of a significant interest mismatch.

Credit risk

Disclosure of credit risk enables the user of financial information to assess the extent to which failures by counterparts to discharge their obligations could reduce the amount of future cash inflows from financial assets on hand at the balance sheet date. In making this assessment, consideration is given to the value of the collateral.

The information required for this assessment can be obtained directly from the balance sheet and related notes with respect to credit risk exposure, such as loans and advances, guarantees and letters of credit.

Fair value

Fair value information is widely used in determining an enterprise's overall financial position and in making decision about individual financial instruments. It is also relevant to many decisions made by users of financial statements since, in many circumstances, it reflects the judgement of the financial markets as to the present value of expected future cash flows relating to an instrument.

With respect to the Bank, the principle determinants of fair value are the expected cash flows, interest rates, exchange rates and credit risk. The fair values of assets and liabilities are not considired to be significantly different from their carrying value.
